# Unit 2 Reflection

[For my study of Unit 2, I began to develop a clearer understanding of the basic design of a website. In Unit 1, I was introduced to the basic knowledge of web pages, which gave me a starting point for learning web development. However, when I moved into Unit 2, I realized that what I had learned in Unit 1 was far from enough for the new requirements and content. Unit 2 was more detailed and systematic, and it helped me see that building a website is not just about putting text or images on a page.

In Unit 2, I started to learn what a complete website should include and what different parts it should have. For example, I learned that a good website needs a clear structure, organized content, and a design that makes it easy for users to understand and use. It is important to think about things like headings, navigation, layout, colors, and the way information is presented. These are not things I fully understood in Unit 1, because at that stage I was only learning the most basic concepts.

This unit also made me realize that web design is more than just writing code. It requires planning and thinking about the user’s experience. A website should not only work correctly, but also look clear, attractive, and easy to use. Unit 2 explained these ideas in a more complete and organized way, which helped me build a stronger foundation for future learning.

Although I still find some parts challenging, I feel that Unit 2 has helped me move from simply knowing a few basic webpage skills to understanding the overall design of a website. It has made me more aware of what makes a website complete and effective, and I believe this will be very helpful for my future studies in web development.]
